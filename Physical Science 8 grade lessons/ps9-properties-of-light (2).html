What Is Light?

Module 1 of 7 · Introduction

Light is a form of electromagnetic energy that travels as waves. It is the only form of energy our eyes can detect directly. Light travels at an incredible speed — approximately 300,000,000 meters per second in a vacuum!

Key Idea: When light strikes a surface or passes into a new material, three things can happen — it can be reflected, refracted, or absorbed. Often all three happen at once!
🔄

Reflection

Light bounces off a surface and changes direction

🌊

Refraction

Light bends as it passes from one material into another

🌑

Absorption

Light energy is taken in by a material and converted to heat

🌈 Visible Light & The Electromagnetic Spectrum

Visible light is just a small portion of the full electromagnetic spectrum. Different colors of visible light have different wavelengths.

Violet (380nm) Blue Green Yellow Orange Red (700nm)

Reflection

Module 2 of 7 · How light bounces

REFLECTION

THE LAW OF REFLECTION

Reflection occurs when light strikes a surface and bounces back. The direction of the reflected ray follows a precise rule:

Law of Reflection: The angle of incidence = the angle of reflection.
Both angles are measured from an imaginary line called the normal — a line perpendicular to the surface at the point where light hits.

🪞 REGULAR (SPECULAR) REFLECTION

Occurs on smooth, shiny surfaces like mirrors or calm water. All light rays reflect at the same angle, producing a clear image.

  • Mirror
  • Polished metal
  • Calm lake surface

📋 DIFFUSE REFLECTION

Occurs on rough surfaces. Light rays hit tiny uneven bumps and scatter in many directions. No clear image is formed.

  • Paper
  • Painted walls
  • Rough asphalt

🔑 WHY CAN WE SEE OBJECTS?

Most objects do not produce their own light. We see them because they diffusely reflect light from another source (like the sun or a lamp) into our eyes. This is called reflected light.

Refraction

Module 3 of 7 · How light bends

REFRACTION

WHAT IS REFRACTION?

Refraction is the bending of light as it passes from one transparent material (medium) into another. The bending occurs because light travels at different speeds in different materials.

Rule: Light bends toward the normal when slowing down (entering a denser medium) and away from the normal when speeding up (entering a less dense medium).

📊 SPEED OF LIGHT IN DIFFERENT MATERIALS

MaterialSpeed of LightRelative Density
Vacuum / Space300,000,000 m/s—
Air~299,700,000 m/sVery low
Water~225,000,000 m/sMedium
Glass~200,000,000 m/sHigh
Diamond~124,000,000 m/sVery high

🌈 DISPERSION

When white light refracts through a prism, different colors bend at slightly different angles, separating into a spectrum. This is how rainbows form!

👁️ REAL-WORLD EXAMPLES

  • Straw appearing bent in water
  • Swimming pool looking shallower
  • Mirages on hot roads
  • Camera and eyeglass lenses

Absorption

Module 4 of 7 · How light is taken in

ABSORPTION

WHAT IS ABSORPTION?

Absorption occurs when light energy strikes a material and is taken in rather than reflected or transmitted. The absorbed energy is usually converted into thermal (heat) energy, causing the object to warm up.

Key Idea: The more light a material absorbs, the warmer it gets. Dark-colored objects absorb more light energy than light-colored objects.

⚫ DARK COLORS

Dark colors absorb most wavelengths of visible light. Black absorbs nearly all incoming light energy, converting it to heat. This is why dark asphalt feels so hot in summer!

⚪ LIGHT COLORS

Light colors reflect most wavelengths of visible light. White reflects almost all visible light, absorbing very little. This is why white clothing keeps you cooler in sunlight.

🎨 COLOR & ABSORPTION — THE CONNECTION

The color we see is the wavelength of light that is reflected, not absorbed. When you see a red apple:

  • The apple absorbs blue, green, orange, and yellow wavelengths
  • The apple reflects red wavelengths into your eyes
  • Your brain interprets that reflected red light as the color "red"
Think about it: A black object absorbs ALL visible wavelengths. A white object reflects ALL visible wavelengths. Colored objects selectively absorb and reflect specific wavelengths.

🌍 REAL-WORLD APPLICATIONS

ApplicationAbsorption Principle
