Chemical Bonding: Self-Paced Lesson for 8th Grade Physical Science

Aligned with Virginia SOL PS.2

Learning Objectives

  • Understand atomic structure - Identify protons, neutrons, and electrons; describe electron arrangement in atoms
  • Define chemical bonds - Explain what chemical bonds are and why atoms form bonds
  • Identify ionic bonds - Describe how ionic bonds form between metals and nonmetals through electron transfer
  • Identify covalent bonds - Describe how covalent bonds form between nonmetals through electron sharing
  • Identify metallic bonds - Describe how metallic bonds form between metal atoms
  • Compare bond types - Distinguish characteristics and differences between the three types of bonds
  • Predict bonding patterns - Use the periodic table to predict what type of bond will form between elements
  • Real-world applications - Connect bonding concepts to everyday materials and substances

Quiz

1. What is a chemical bond?
A) The nucleus of an atom
B) A force of attraction holding atoms or ions together
C) The space between electron shells
D) A type of chemical reaction

Answer: B

2. Ionic bonds form between:
A) Two nonmetals
B) Two metals
C) A metal and a nonmetal
D) Any combination of elements

Answer: C

3. Covalent bonds form between:
A) A metal and a nonmetal
B) Two nonmetals
C) A metal and a metalloid
D) Two metals

Answer: B

4. Metallic bonds typically form between:
A) Metal and nonmetal atoms
B) Two nonmetals
C) Metal atoms themselves
D) Positive and negative ions

Answer: C

5. In an ionic bond, what process occurs?
A) Electrons are shared equally
B) Electrons are shared unequally
C) Electrons are transferred from one atom to another
D) Electrons are created

Answer: C
