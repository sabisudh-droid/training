Human Impact & Conservation

Examine how human activities affect ecosystems and what Virginia is doing to protect its natural heritage.

📚 VA SOL BIO.9a, BIO.9b, BIO.9c
🏭
Slide 1 of 4
Major Human Impacts
  • Habitat loss — #1 cause of biodiversity loss; deforestation, urban sprawl
  • Pollution — air, water, soil; affects food chains
  • Invasive species — outcompete native species
  • Overexploitation — overfishing, hunting, poaching
  • Climate change — shifts biomes, disrupts cycles
🌡️
Slide 2 of 4
Climate Change & Ecosystems
Rising CO₂ → greenhouse effect → global warming:

  • Rising sea levels threaten coastal Virginia wetlands
  • Shifting temperatures change species' ranges
  • Ocean acidification harms shellfish in Chesapeake Bay
  • Earlier springs disrupt migration and breeding timing
🛡️
Slide 3 of 4
Conservation Strategies
  • Protected areas — national/state parks, wildlife refuges
  • Habitat restoration — reforestation, wetland restoration
  • Captive breeding — for endangered species
  • Laws & policy — Endangered Species Act, Clean Water Act
  • Sustainable practices — responsible fishing, renewable energy
🦋
Slide 4 of 4
Virginia Conservation Efforts
Virginia is actively protecting its ecosystems:

  • Chesapeake Bay Program — multi-state restoration effort
  • Virginia Wildlife Action Plan — 1,000+ species of concern
  • Shenandoah National Park — 200,000+ acres protected
  • Native Plant Program — restore native species diversity
