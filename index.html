Plan Overviews

Cell Structure and Function (BIO.3)

Five-lesson sequence on cell theory, organelles, membranes, mitosis, and differentiation. Includes videos, slides, labs, assignments, and a 10-question quiz.

Genetics and Heredity (BIO.4)

Mendelian genetics, Punnett squares, probability, and inheritance patterns. Students build pedigrees and analyze dominant vs recessive traits.

Ecology and Systems (BIO.8)

Energy flow, food webs, biogeochemical cycles, and population dynamics. Emphasizes human impact and ecosystem stability.

Photosynthesis and Cellular Respiration (BIO.5)

ATP production, chemical equations, and organelle roles. Hands-on investigations compare plant and animal energy pathways.

DNA, RNA & Protein Synthesis (BIO.6)

Covers DNA structure, semi-conservative replication, transcription, translation, and mutation types. Includes an interactive transcription/translation simulator and a 10-question quiz.

Evolution & Natural Selection (BIO.7)

Covers Darwin's four postulates, types of natural selection, and multiple lines of evidence including fossil records and molecular homology. Students run a peppered moth simulation and complete an interactive 10-question quiz.

Biological Molecules and Enzymes (BIO.2)

Macromolecules, enzyme structure/function, and reaction rates. Lab activities explore pH, temperature, and substrate specificity.

Human Body Systems (BIO.9)

Covers all major organ systems — circulatory, respiratory, digestive, nervous, and more — with a focus on structure, function, and homeostasis. Includes videos, vocab, and a quiz.

Scientific Investigation and Lab Skills (BIO.1)

Experimental design, variables, measurement, graphing, and claims-evidence-reasoning. Establishes core lab routines and safety.
